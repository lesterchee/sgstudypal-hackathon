# WXW v WXX [2025] SGHC(A) 2

## Case Summary: Adjustment of ANJ v ANK Ratio in Dual-Income Marriages

In the recent Appellate Division of the High Court decision *WXW v WXX [2025] SGHC(A) 2*, the court clarified the classification of a marriage as either "single-income" or "dual-income" for the purposes of dividing matrimonial assets under Section 112 of the Women's Charter.

### Key Rulings on Dual-Income Classification

1.  **Actual Roles vs. Financial Earnings**: The court emphasized that the classification of a marriage as "dual-income" or "single-income" depends on the actual roles and contributions undertaken by the spouses during the marriage, rather than solely on the magnitude of their financial earnings. 
2.  **Application of the ANJ Approach**: Even in marriages classified as dual-income, if one party was less successful financially but made substantial contributions to homemaking, the "ANJ approach" (derived from *ANJ v ANK*) remains a fair and structured method for asset division. 
3.  **Recognizing Substantial Homemaking**: The judgment highlights that the court will use the ANJ formula to fairly recognize both parties' contributions and achieve a just outcome, particularly valuing the substantial homemaking efforts of a spouse whose financial earnings may have been comparatively lower in a dual-income context.

### Impact on Precedent

This case reinforces that the *ANJ v ANK* formula is adaptable and highly relevant not just in traditional single-income marriages, but also in dual-income scenarios where one spouse takes on a disproportionately larger share of homemaking duties. It ensures that significant indirect non-financial contributions (homemaking, childcare) are equitably valued against direct financial contributions.
